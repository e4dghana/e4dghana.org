Our Target Groups

People at the Centre of Every Programme

E4D Ghana's work is deliberately targeted — we focus our energy and resources on the groups most often left behind by mainstream development. Our four target groups are not statistics: they are the farmers, young mothers, school dropouts, and aspiring entrepreneurs whose lives we are committed to improving alongside.

Children

Supporting the well-being, protection, and holistic development of children across Northern Ghana.

Children in Northern Ghana face compounded disadvantages — high poverty rates, limited school infrastructure, early marriage pressures, and inadequate child protection systems. E4D Ghana works to ensure every child has the safety, opportunity, and nurturing environment they need to grow, learn, and thrive. Our child-focused programming spans literacy support, child rights awareness, and community-level protection advocacy.

Youth including NEETs

Empowering young people — including those not in education, employment, or training — with skills, platforms, and pathways to a better future.

NEETs — young people Not in Education, Employment, or Training — represent one of the most visible yet underserved populations in Northern Ghana. Many have dropped out of school, failed to access the job market, or lack the skills and networks to pursue self-employment. E4D Ghana targets this group through vocational training, entrepreneurship support, mentorship, digital literacy, and civic engagement — creating multiple pathways into productive and purposeful lives.

Women

Advancing gender equity through targeted programmes that empower women economically, socially, and politically.

Women in Northern Ghana face a unique combination of barriers — limited access to land, finance, and markets; under-representation in leadership; and social norms that constrain their agency. E4D Ghana is committed to dismantling these barriers through targeted economic empowerment, financial literacy, women's leadership development, and advocacy for gender-responsive policies. We believe no community can thrive unless its women are fully included, heard, and resourced.

Individuals with No or Low Skills

Providing capacity-building and vocational training to improve livelihoods for individuals with no or low formal skills.

Across Northern Ghana, a significant proportion of the adult population — including returning migrants, persons with disabilities, and those who have never accessed formal education — lack the skills required to participate meaningfully in the economy. E4D Ghana provides contextualised, practical skills training in trades, agribusiness, digital tools, and enterprise development — meeting people where they are and equipping them with what they need to build dignified, sustainable livelihoods.
