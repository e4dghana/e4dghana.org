Our Mission

To work for and with children, women, young people and individuals with low skills and our partners to cause positive changes at individual and systemic levels.

Our Vision

A society where children, women, young people (including NEETs) and individuals with low skills are empowered to contribute meaningfully to sustainable national and global development.

Our Target Groups

The communities and individuals at the heart of everything we do.

Children

Supporting the well-being, protection, and development of children across Northern Ghana.

Youth including NEETs

Empowering young people, including those not in education, employment, or training (NEETs), with skills and opportunities.

Individuals with No or Low Skills

Providing capacity-building and vocational training for individuals with no or low skills to improve their livelihoods.

Women

Advancing gender equity through targeted programmes that empower women economically, socially, and politically.

Our Intervention Areas

Five interconnected pillars driving youth-led change across Northern Ghana.

Education & Lifelong Learning

Promoting access to quality education and continuous learning opportunities for all ages, empowering individuals to grow and adapt throughout life.

ICT & Digital Literacy

Bridging the digital divide through technology training, coding workshops, and digital skills development to prepare communities for the modern economy.

Social Entrepreneurship & Youth Employment

Equipping young people with entrepreneurial skills, business mentorship, and employment pathways to build sustainable livelihoods.

Environmental Sustainability & Climate Change

Leading community-driven climate adaptation, environmental conservation, and awareness campaigns to build resilient and sustainable communities.

Ethical Leadership, Civic Engagement & Peacebuilding

Fostering responsible leadership, active civic participation, dialogue, and conflict resolution for peaceful, cohesive communities across Northern Ghana.

Upcoming Initiatives Seeking Funding & Partnerships

Empower for Development Ghana has co-created and designed the following initiatives to advance our vision and mission. We are actively seeking funding and partnerships to bring these interventions to life.

Seeking Partners 01
YACA

Youth Accelerate Climate Action

A youth-led climate education and advocacy initiative aimed at strengthening climate literacy and community resilience in rural Northern Ghana. The project will train young people as Climate Action Ambassadors to promote climate-smart and sustainable agricultural practices among farmers in selected communities of the Kumbungu District.

Through community dialogues, storytelling sessions, training workshops, and social media advocacy, YACA seeks to empower youth leadership in climate action while supporting farmers to better adapt to climate change and environmental challenges.

Kumbungu District Climate Education Youth-Led Community Dialogues
Seeking Partners 02
RESTORE–Ghana · Resilient Engagement for Social Trust, Opportunity, Reconciliation & Empowerment

RESTORE–Ghana

A youth-led peace and climate resilience initiative that brings together farmers, Fulbe herders, youth, women, and community leaders in conflict-affected communities in the Mion, Karaga and Gushegu districts, Northern Region.

Through intergenerational storytelling, restorative healing circles, and community sports, the project creates safe spaces for dialogue, healing, and connection — reducing farmer–herder tensions and strengthening social cohesion.

Mion · Karaga · Gushegu Peacebuilding Healing & Wellbeing

Seeking Partners 03
RESILIENT–Ghana

RESILIENT–Ghana

A youth-led climate justice and peacebuilding initiative that will address climate-driven tensions between farmers and Fulbe herders in selected districts in Northern Ghana.

The project will train Climate Justice Ambassadors and mobilise communities through intergenerational dialogues, restorative healing circles, and community sports to promote climate literacy, sustainable natural resource use, and peaceful coexistence.

By empowering youth and strengthening collaboration among farmers, herders, and community leaders, RESILIENT–Ghana will help build resilient communities, reduce resource-based conflicts, and advance climate adaptation.
